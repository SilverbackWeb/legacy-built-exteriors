Common Questions from South Riding Homeowners

My home was built in the 1990s in South Riding. What should I prioritize?

Homes from the early-to-mid 1990s in South Riding are now 30 or more years old, which puts them in the range where roofing systems, wood soffit and fascia boards, and original gutters are frequently in need of replacement. A free comprehensive exterior inspection is the right starting point — we'll assess each system and help you prioritize based on condition, urgency, and your budget.

Does Legacy Built Exteriors handle South Riding HOA approvals?

Yes. South Riding has an active homeowners association with architectural standards covering roofing and siding materials and colors. We assist homeowners in identifying compliant material options, preparing their HOA application, and following up as needed. We will not begin installation until you have written approval from the HOA.

What's causing my gutters to overflow even though they're not clogged?

Overflowing gutters that aren't blocked are usually the result of gutters that were undersized for the roof area they're draining, gutters that have sagged and lost their pitch, or downspouts that are too small or spaced too far apart. South Riding's hills mean water moves quickly during heavy rain events, and gutters that were marginal when installed often can't keep up. We'll diagnose the specific issue and provide a solution.

In many cases, yes — particularly for localized storm damage or impact damage.
