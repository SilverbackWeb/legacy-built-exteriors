Common Questions from Burke Homeowners

My Burke home is from the 1980s. Is now a good time to replace my roof?

If your home's roof was last replaced in the 1980s or early 1990s, you're likely at or past the typical 30-35 year lifespan for architectural shingles from that era. Signs to watch for include shingles that are curling at the edges, granule accumulation in gutters, dark streaking on the roof surface, and any interior signs of moisture intrusion. We offer free inspections to give you an honest assessment of whether replacement is needed now or can wait another few years.

Does the Burke Centre Conservancy need to approve exterior changes?

Burke Centre Conservancy has an Architectural Review process for some exterior modifications, particularly for material changes or color changes that alter the home's appearance. Roof replacements using similar shingles typically don't require review, but siding replacements — especially material changes — often do. We help homeowners prepare review submissions and have experience working within Burke Centre's guidelines.

What's the best gutter solution for a wooded Burke lot?

For heavily wooded lots in Burke, we typically recommend seamless aluminum gutters properly sized for the roof's drainage load, combined with a quality gutter guard system. The guard system dramatically reduces how often gutters need cleaning — typically from twice or three times per year to once every few years — while still allowing water to flow freely. We'll assess your specific tree situation and recommend a solution that balances performance and cost.
