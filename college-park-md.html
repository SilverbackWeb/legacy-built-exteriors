Why College Park Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

College Park's dual identity — a residential community and a university town — creates a housing market where property condition varies enormously from block to block. In established owner-occupied neighborhoods like Calvert Hills, Old Town, and Hollywood, homeowners have generally maintained their properties well and are often looking to upgrade materials to modern standards. In areas with higher rental concentration, deferred maintenance is common, and what may appear to be a routine roofing or gutter job can sometimes reveal underlying issues that need to be addressed at the same time.

College Park's older homes — particularly those in the historic Old Town area — were built in an era when wood was the universal exterior material. Many still have original or early-replacement wood soffits, wood window trim, and wood fascia that have been painted over repeatedly but are now at the end of their service life. Replacing these components with low-maintenance modern materials is an investment that eliminates recurring paint and repair costs for decades.

College Park's position between two major traffic corridors — Route 1 and the Capital Beltway — means many homes are within a few blocks of significant road noise and activity. The density of the surrounding area also means project logistics require careful planning — particularly for properties with limited driveway access or tight setbacks from neighboring structures. Our crews are experienced working in these conditions efficiently.

Do you work with investment property owners and landlords, not just homeowners?

I have an older home in College Park with potential lead paint on the exterior. How do you handle that?

Homes built before 1978 may have lead-based paint on exterior surfaces. When disturbing painted surfaces on pre-1978 homes, EPA RRP (Renovation, Repair, and Painting) rules apply. Our team follows proper procedures for containment and disposal when working on older homes. If you're concerned about lead paint, we can discuss your specific situation and what protocols we follow.

My gutters seem to overflow during heavy rain even though they're not clogged. What causes this?

Overflowing gutters that aren't clogged typically indicate that the gutter is undersized for the roof area it's draining, or that the slope (pitch) toward the downspout is insufficient. Many older College Park homes were built with 4-inch gutters that simply can't handle the volume of water from today's intense rain events. Upgrading to 5-inch or 6-inch seamless gutters with properly placed downspouts is usually the right solution — and we size and slope every system we install based on the actual roof area and local rainfall data.
