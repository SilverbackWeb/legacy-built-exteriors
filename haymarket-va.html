Common Questions from Haymarket Homeowners

My Dominion Valley home was built around 2005 — should I be thinking about a new roof?

A 2005 home is now 20+ years old — right at the threshold where architectural shingles from that era start showing significant wear. We recommend a professional inspection to assess granule loss, flashing integrity, and attic ventilation. If the roof is performing well and properly maintained, it may have another 5-10 years of useful life. If it's showing advanced wear, planning for replacement now avoids the risk of leaks and interior damage from a failed system.

Does Piedmont's HOA need to approve new roofing or siding?

Piedmont's HOA maintains an Architectural Review Committee that reviews significant exterior changes. Roofing replacements using similar shingle colors typically have a streamlined process, but material upgrades — like switching from vinyl to James Hardie — require formal review with color and product documentation. We help homeowners prepare complete applications that include everything the ARC needs to approve quickly.

Are there wind-specific roofing considerations for Haymarket?

Yes. Haymarket's elevation near the Bull Run Mountains increases wind exposure, and we specify higher wind-resistance shingles for homes in exposed locations. Architectural shingles with Class H wind ratings (130 mph) are available and worth considering for Haymarket's hillier sites. Proper starter strip installation and adequate fastening are also critical — we don't cut corners on these details.
