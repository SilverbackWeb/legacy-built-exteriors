Why Gaithersburg Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Gaithersburg's housing market spans over a century of development, but much of its current stock was built during two distinct booms: the postwar era of the 1960s and 1970s, and the planned community development of the 1990s and 2000s. Homes from the earlier era often have aging wood siding, outdated roofing, and undersized gutter systems that can't handle today's increasingly intense storm events. Kentlands, Lakelands, and similar newer developments are now 20–30 years old — old enough that original roofing and siding is reaching the end of its designed lifespan.

Gaithersburg's location in upper Montgomery County also means slightly higher elevation and greater exposure to winter weather than communities closer to DC. Ice dam formation on low-slope roof sections is a recurring issue we address regularly. Proper attic insulation and ventilation — often improved during a re-roofing project — can significantly reduce ice dam risk in subsequent winters.

Many Gaithersburg communities, particularly Kentlands, have active HOAs with architectural review requirements. We're experienced working within these frameworks and can advise on material and color selections that will meet approval while delivering the performance and longevity you need.

Common Questions from Gaithersburg Homeowners

My Kentlands HOA requires approval before exterior work — how does that process work?

Kentlands and Lakelands HOAs require homeowner submissions to their Architectural Review Committee before most exterior work begins. We can assist by providing material specifications, color samples, and product cut sheets to support your application. We've worked with Kentlands homeowners through this process before and are familiar with commonly approved materials and profiles.

How does Gaithersburg's winter weather affect roofing systems?

Gaithersburg's slightly higher elevation in upper Montgomery County means more frequent and heavier ice and snow accumulation compared to communities closer to DC. Ice dams — where ice forms at roof edges and causes water to back up under shingles — are a recurring issue on homes with poor attic insulation or insufficient ventilation. When we replace a roof, we evaluate and address the underlying ventilation system, which is often the real cause of ice dam problems.

Is a 25-year-old roof in Gaithersburg always ready for replacement?

Age is just one factor. A 25-year-old roof with proper ventilation, minimal storm damage, and no major leak history may have years of service life remaining. Conversely, a 15-year-old roof that was poorly installed or sits under heavy tree coverage with moss growth may already be failing. Our free inspections include a detailed condition assessment — we'll give you our honest professional opinion rather than a blanket recommendation for replacement.

What are the advantages of seamless gutters over sectional gutters?

Sectional gutters have seams every few feet that are common failure points for leaks — especially after freeze-thaw cycling in Maryland winters. Seamless gutters are formed on-site as a single continuous piece, eliminating seam failures almost entirely. They also look cleaner and require less maintenance. We install seamless aluminum gutters exclusively and size them properly for each home's drainage requirements.

How long does James Hardie siding installation take on a Gaithersburg home?

A typical single-family home siding installation takes three to seven days depending on home size, complexity of trim work, and the number of stories. Homes with significant architectural detail or custom trim profiles take longer. We provide timeline estimates before the project starts and keep homeowners informed throughout. Most Gaithersburg projects are completed with minimal disruption to daily routines.
