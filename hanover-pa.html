Why Hanover Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Hanover sits in the southeastern corner of York County at an elevation where it catches cold air funneling down from the higher terrain to the north and west. The town regularly records more freeze-thaw events per winter than areas just 30 miles south — and each cycle of freezing and thawing works on roofing and siding materials like a slow mechanical stress test. Flashings pull loose, caulk joints crack, and shingles that were already at the end of their life shed their granules faster under repeated thermal cycling.

The town's mature tree canopy — particularly the large hardwoods lining streets in the historic neighborhoods — drops significant leaf and debris volume into gutters each fall. Gutters that fill and overflow in late autumn set up conditions for foundation water infiltration and ice buildup at the eaves once temperatures drop in December and January. Gutter protection systems, combined with properly sized downspouts, are a meaningful investment for Hanover homeowners.

Hanover also lies close to the Mason-Dixon line, which means its summers are nearly as warm and humid as Maryland's. That humidity accelerates the degradation of wood siding, promotes algae and mold growth on roofing surfaces, and contributes to the kind of moisture infiltration through the building envelope that leads to insulation and structural issues over time. Materials and installation methods appropriate for this climate zone are critical to long-term exterior performance.

My Hanover home has older T1-11 siding that is rotting. What do you recommend?

T1-11 panel siding was widely installed on homes built in the 1960s through 1980s throughout York County, and it rarely holds up beyond 30 to 40 years in Pennsylvania's climate. Once it begins retaining moisture and rotting, there is no effective way to stop the progression. We recommend replacing it with James Hardie fiber cement siding — a product specifically engineered for humid mid-Atlantic climates that will not rot, warp, or crack across the full range of Hanover temperatures.

What is the best time of year to schedule roofing or siding work in Hanover?

Late spring through early fall tends to offer the most favorable installation conditions, but we complete roofing and siding projects year-round. Asphalt shingles do require temperatures above about 40 degrees for proper sealing, which can limit some winter scheduling. The best approach is to get on our schedule as early as possible — demand typically peaks in late spring, and homeowners who schedule in winter or early spring tend to get their preferred start dates.

How do I know whether I need a full roof replacement or just repairs?

This is one of the most common questions we receive, and the honest answer depends on the age of the roof, the extent of the damage, and the condition of the underlying deck. A 15-year-old roof with a few failed shingles may be a good candidate for repair. A 25-year-old roof showing granule loss across multiple areas, soft spots, or widespread flashing failure is typically better served by replacement — repairs on aged roofing often mask the full problem.
