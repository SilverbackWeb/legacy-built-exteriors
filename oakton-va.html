Common Questions from Oakton Homeowners

How do I know if my Oakton home's roof needs replacement versus repair?

The key factors are age, the extent of wear, and the cost-benefit of repair versus replacement. If your roof is under 15 years old and damage is isolated — a small section of missing shingles or a failed flashing detail — repair is usually appropriate. If the roof is 20 years or older, shows widespread granule loss, has multiple areas of curling or cupping shingles, or has been repaired multiple times, a full replacement is almost always more economical in the medium term. A free inspection from our team gives you a clear picture of the roof's current condition and honest guidance on the right path forward.

What is involved in a Fairfax County roofing permit for an Oakton home?

Fairfax County requires a building permit for all full roof replacements, which involves submitting project details including contractor information, material specifications, and property address. Once approved, the work must be inspected at key stages. The permit ensures the installation meets Virginia Residential Code requirements for underlayment, ventilation, flashings, and fastening patterns. We handle the entire process — application, fee payment, inspection scheduling — at no additional charge to you as part of every roofing project in Oakton.

My Oakton home has a two-story colonial — how long does a full roofing project typically take?

A typical two-story colonial in Oakton with a standard gable or hip roof of 25–30 squares can usually be completed in one full day by our crew. Homes with more complex rooflines — multiple dormers, skylights, attached garage with a separate pitch — may extend to a day and a half or two days. We always provide a realistic timeframe specific to your home's size and complexity before scheduling, so you can plan accordingly.

Can you add attic ventilation when replacing my roof?

Yes, and in many cases we recommend it. Adequate attic ventilation is critical to roof longevity — it prevents heat and moisture buildup that accelerates shingle aging from beneath. On Oakton homes where we find inadequate ridge ventilation or blocked soffit venting, we can add or upgrade ridge vents, install powered attic ventilators where needed, and ensure soffit vents are clear and functioning. Improving ventilation during a roof replacement extends the life of the new shingles and can reduce cooling loads in summer.
