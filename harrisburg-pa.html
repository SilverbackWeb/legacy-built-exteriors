Siding

Harrisburg's older housing stock, including the dense rowhouse neighborhoods close to downtown, often features original brick facades that are fine — but the frame additions, rear walls, and dormer structures frequently carry deteriorated wood siding. Suburban homes from the 1970s through 1990s typically have aging vinyl or wood siding that has reached the end of its service life. James Hardie fiber cement is our recommendation for any home where long-term performance and minimal maintenance matter.

Gutters

Harrisburg's position along the river and its significant annual rainfall — around 40 inches per year — makes functional gutters essential, not optional. The city's older homes frequently have undersized gutters that overflow in heavy rain and freeze solid in winter, creating dangerous ice at the eaves and saturating soil against foundations. We install properly sized, seamless aluminum gutter systems with downspout placement designed for each home's specific drainage conditions.

Soffits & Fascia

In Harrisburg's older neighborhoods, deteriorated soffit and fascia is one of the most common and most overlooked exterior problems we encounter. Rotted wood in these areas creates open channels for pests — birds, squirrels, and insects — to access attic spaces, while also eliminating the cross-ventilation that keeps attics dry in summer and prevents ice dams in winter. We replace failing systems with durable aluminum or fiber cement that closes the envelope and restores proper airflow.

Why Harrisburg Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Harrisburg's geography creates a unique weather exposure profile. The city sits in the Susquehanna River valley, where cold air pools in winter and humidity concentrates in summer. Storm systems tracking up the East Coast or through the Great Lakes tend to funnel precipitation directly through the region, meaning Harrisburg homeowners experience more snow events, more ice storms, and more freeze-thaw transitions per winter than cities at similar latitudes that are not situated in river valleys. These conditions are hard on every exterior system — roofing, siding, gutters, and soffits alike.

The city's extensive stock of pre-war rowhouses presents particular exterior challenges. These homes were built with construction practices that are very different from modern standards — smaller roof overhangs that accelerate wall wetting, limited attic ventilation, and original drainage systems designed for a time when rainfall intensity was lower. Updating these homes' exterior systems to perform reliably in today's climate requires understanding how they were built and how they have aged, not just replacing the visible surface materials.

Harrisburg's suburban ring in Dauphin County and across the river on the Cumberland County West Shore presents a different profile: mid-century ranch and split-level homes and 1980s and 1990s colonials, many of which are now at the age where roofing, siding, and gutter systems need full replacement. These projects are more straightforward in construction terms, but they are no less important — and the quality of materials and installation is what determines whether the next system lasts 15 years or 35.

Common Questions from Harrisburg Homeowners

Do you work on city rowhouses and attached homes in Harrisburg, or only detached single-family homes?

We work on the full range of Harrisburg housing types, including rowhouses, semi-detached homes, and townhouses throughout the city's historic neighborhoods. Rowhouse and attached-home exterior projects require additional coordination — particularly for roofing, where shared walls and party wall situations affect how work is scoped and sequenced. We are experienced with these situations and communicate clearly about what can and cannot be addressed within the scope of your individual property.

My Harrisburg home's roof has a flat or low-slope section. Can you handle that?

Yes. Many of Harrisburg's older homes include flat or low-slope roof sections over additions, porches, and rear shed dormers. These require different waterproofing approaches than steep-slope asphalt shingle systems. We work with modified bitumen and TPO systems for low-slope applications and can integrate these seamlessly with the pitched portions of a roof. Proper drainage design is critical on flat roofs, and we pay close attention to the drain or scupper placement to prevent ponding.

How do Harrisburg's winters compare to other parts of Pennsylvania for roofing durability?

Harrisburg sits in a geographic position that makes it particularly susceptible to ice storm events — the zone where precipitation transitions between rain, freezing rain, sleet, and snow depending on small temperature variations. This means Harrisburg roofs frequently experience ice accumulation and freeze-thaw cycling in a single storm event, which is hard on flashings, sealants, and granule adhesion on aging shingles. Proper material selection and ice-and-water shield placement are especially important here.

Is James Hardie siding appropriate for Harrisburg's older homes?

James Hardie fiber cement siding is one of the best choices for older Harrisburg homes that are transitioning away from deteriorated wood siding. It is primed and pre-painted at the factory or job site, holds color significantly longer than wood, and will not rot, absorb moisture, or crack in hard freezes. For homes in Harrisburg's historic districts, Hardie's lap siding profiles can closely replicate the look of original wood clapboard while providing dramatically better performance. We can help navigate historic review requirements as needed.

What should I do if my roof was damaged in a storm — can I file an insurance claim?

If your Harrisburg home sustained storm damage, documenting the damage and filing a claim with your homeowners insurance carrier is often the right first step. We can provide a detailed damage assessment report that supports the insurance process, and we are experienced working alongside insurance adjusters on storm damage claims. We give you an honest assessment of what was storm-related and what was pre-existing wear — and we will not overstate damage to inflate a claim.
