Why Broadlands Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Broadlands was developed with a strong emphasis on landscaping, tree preservation, and green space — which contributes to its highly desirable character but also creates specific maintenance demands for homeowners. The mature trees throughout the community mean gutters fill with leaf and seed debris quickly in fall, and the shade on certain elevations can keep moisture on roofing surfaces longer than it would on a more open lot. Over time, this promotes algae and moss growth that accelerates shingle wear.

The Broadlands HOA maintains active architectural standards that govern exterior material types, colors, and styles. Any roofing or siding project requires HOA approval before work begins. Legacy Built Exteriors is thoroughly familiar with these requirements and will guide you through the process — from identifying compliant materials to preparing your application to the architectural review board.

The community's mix of townhomes and single-family homes also creates different project parameters depending on your property type. Townhome owners sometimes need to coordinate with adjacent unit owners or obtain additional approvals. We have experience managing these situations efficiently and professionally.

Common Questions from Broadlands Homeowners

How does Legacy Built Exteriors handle the Broadlands HOA approval process?

We help you prepare all required documentation for the Broadlands HOA architectural review board, including material specifications, color samples, and project descriptions. We wait for written approval before scheduling any installation work, so you won't face the risk of post-project violations or required do-overs.

I have algae staining on my Broadlands roof. Does that mean I need a replacement?

Not necessarily. Algae staining by itself doesn't always indicate end-of-life shingles — it's common in shaded, wooded communities like Broadlands where moisture lingers on roof surfaces. However, algae growth can accelerate granule loss and shingle wear over time. We'll inspect the overall condition of your shingles and let you know whether treatment and prevention or a full replacement makes more sense for your situation.

Can you match the siding color currently on my Broadlands home?

In many cases, yes. We work with a wide range of James Hardie colors and can often match or closely approximate existing siding colors. For full siding replacements, you'll select new colors subject to HOA approval. We carry samples and can help you choose compliant options that complement your home's architecture.
