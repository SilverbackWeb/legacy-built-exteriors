Why Herndon Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Herndon's housing stock spans a broad range of construction eras, and each era brings its own set of common exterior issues. Homes from the 1970s and 80s frequently have original aluminum or early vinyl siding that has faded, dented, or cracked, along with wood soffit and fascia systems that have absorbed moisture over decades. Common Questions from Herndon Homeowners

Is the Town of Herndon permitting different from Fairfax County for roofing?

Yes. Properties within the incorporated limits of the Town of Herndon use the town's own permit office, while homes in the surrounding unincorporated Herndon area are permitted through Fairfax County. We verify the correct jurisdiction for every Herndon project and handle all permits and inspections appropriately — you don't need to figure out which system applies to your address.

My Herndon home's vinyl siding is from the early 1990s — is James Hardie a practical upgrade?

Absolutely. Early-90s vinyl siding is typically 30 years or more old — well past its practical lifespan in Northern Virginia's climate. It's likely showing fading, cracking at corners, and deterioration around window and door trim. Replacing it with James Hardie fiber cement provides dramatically superior durability, better moisture resistance, a much more substantial appearance, and paint retention that eliminates the repainting cycle required with wood. It's one of the most impactful upgrades available for a mid-era Herndon home.

How does wind affect roofing performance near Dulles Airport?

The western Fairfax County area, including Herndon, does experience higher prevailing wind speeds than areas closer to D.C., partly due to open terrain near Dulles. For roofing installations, this means proper fastening patterns matter significantly — installing shingles to the manufacturer's high-wind specification, with six nails per shingle rather than four where required, and ensuring starter strips are properly sealed at eaves and rakes. We spec and install to these standards as a default on all Herndon projects.

Can you replace my gutters without replacing the fascia first?

It depends on the condition of the fascia. If the fascia boards are sound — no soft spots, no rot, paint still adhering well — new gutters can be attached directly to existing fascia. However, if the fascia has any rot or is soft to the touch, we strongly recommend replacing it before hanging new gutters. Installing gutters into rotted fascia is a short-term fix; the new gutters will pull away within a few seasons and the underlying damage will worsen. We assess fascia condition as a standard part of every gutter estimate in Herndon.

What is the warranty on James Hardie siding installed by Legacy Built Exteriors?

James Hardie's fiber cement siding products come with a 30-year non-prorated limited warranty on the substrate. Pre-finished HardieColor products carry a 15-year fade and chip warranty on the factory finish. These warranties are backed by James Hardie directly and are among the strongest substrate warranties available for any siding product. As a James Hardie installer, we also provide our own workmanship warranty on the installation itself — covering labor and fastening quality. We review the full warranty details during your estimate.
