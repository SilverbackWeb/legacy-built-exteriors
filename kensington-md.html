Why Kensington Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Kensington's Victorian-era downtown and surrounding neighborhoods contain some of the oldest and most architecturally distinctive homes in Montgomery County. Homes built in the late 1800s and early 1900s often have complex rooflines with multiple dormers, valleys, and original decorative woodwork that requires careful attention during exterior work. These features are part of what makes Kensington homes valuable — and they require a contractor who understands how to work with them, not around them.

The area's mature trees create constant challenges for gutters and roofs alike. Shingle deterioration from shade and organic debris is accelerated in Kensington compared to more open communities, and gutters require regular attention to prevent the overflow that can damage the period-appropriate foundations and landscaping Kensington homeowners take pride in maintaining.

Town of Kensington homes may also be subject to specific preservation guidelines depending on their location. We're familiar with the area's character preservation priorities and can recommend materials that are both functionally superior and visually appropriate for the historic streetscape.

Common Questions from Kensington Homeowners

Can fiber cement siding really match the look of my Victorian-era home?

Yes. James Hardie offers HardieShingle — a fiber cement product that precisely replicates the look of cedar shake shingles common on Victorian-era homes in Kensington — as well as beaded profiles and wide-exposure lap siding that complement older architectural styles. Factory-applied ColorPlus finishes are available in historically appropriate palettes. Many Kensington homeowners are surprised at how closely Hardie profiles match their original wood siding.

How should I handle roofing on a home with multiple dormers and a complex roofline?

Complex rooflines require more careful planning and more skilled installation than simple gable roofs. Valley intersections, dormers, and transitions between roof planes are the most common points of failure. Our experienced crews are comfortable with the intricate flashing and detailing these rooflines demand. We provide detailed proposals that account for every element of the roof system, not just the field shingles.

My Kensington home has wide decorative overhangs — can those be preserved during a soffit replacement?

Absolutely. Wide decorative overhangs are a defining feature of many Kensington homes, and we install replacement soffit and fascia materials in a way that maintains the original proportions and visual character. We use aluminum systems that can be cut and fit to match complex profiles, and we discuss the existing appearance with homeowners before any work begins to ensure the finished result honors the home's architecture.

Are there any preservation restrictions I should know about before replacing my siding?

Homes within the Town of Kensington's historic district area may be subject to local review requirements for exterior alterations. We recommend checking with the town before beginning any siding work. We can assist with documentation and material specifications to support the review process, and we are familiar with the types of materials that meet preservation standards in historic Maryland communities.
