Common Questions from Centreville Homeowners

My Centreville home was built in the 1990s — should I be thinking about roof replacement?

Homes built in the late 1980s and 1990s with standard three-tab or early architectural shingles are now 30-35 years old. That's at or past the expected lifespan for those products. If your roof has never been replaced, or was replaced once in the early 2000s with similar shingles, it's worth having a professional inspection to assess current condition and establish a timeline for replacement before problems develop.

Is Centreville in Fairfax County or Prince William County?

Centreville spans both — the older parts of the community are in Fairfax County, while newer development along the Route 29 and 28 corridors extends into Prince William County. Permit requirements differ between the two jurisdictions. We're experienced with both and will handle the correct application for your specific address as part of every project.

What's the most common exterior issue you see on Centreville homes?

The most common issue we find on inspections in Centreville is shingle granule loss combined with inadequate attic ventilation. When attic heat builds up in summer, it accelerates shingle degradation from below — homeowners see normal aging from outside but don't realize the roof is failing faster than it should. We assess ventilation as part of every roof inspection and correct it when needed as part of the replacement project.
