Why Arlington Homes Have Unique Exterior Needs

Arlington County is one of the most densely developed counties in Virginia, with a housing landscape unlike most of the surrounding suburbs. The county has very little undeveloped land left, which means the homes here are largely older, established properties — and with age comes wear on every exterior surface.

The county's urban forest is exceptional — mature hardwoods create shade and character but also deposit significant debris on roofs year-round. The combination of leaf and branch accumulation, ice damming in winter, and the intense summer thunderstorms that roll through the DC metro area creates a demanding environment for any roof or exterior system.

Arlington also has specific permit and inspection requirements for exterior work, and some neighborhoods have architectural review guidelines. We handle all permitting as part of every project and are fully licensed in Virginia (VA License #2705178937), so there are no surprises for the homeowner.

Common Questions from Arlington Homeowners

Do I need a permit for roof replacement in Arlington County?

Yes — Arlington County requires a building permit for full roof replacements. Repairs under a certain threshold may not require one, but full re-roofing does. We handle all permit applications and inspections as part of our standard process, so this is never something you need to manage yourself.

My Arlington home was built in the 1950s. Are there special considerations?

Absolutely. Homes from this era were built with different framing standards, often have original roof decking that needs evaluation, and may have layered shingles from previous re-roofing jobs. We inspect the decking condition before finalizing a quote and address any underlying issues — not just the surface.

How do I know if storm damage qualifies for an insurance claim in Virginia?

The DC metro area sees significant storm activity — high winds, hail, and heavy rain can all cause insurance-eligible damage even when it's not visually obvious from the ground. We offer free inspections and will document any damage thoroughly. If your roof or siding qualifies for a claim, we'll work directly with your insurance adjuster throughout the process.

Does James Hardie siding work well in Northern Virginia's climate?

It's actually ideal for this region. Northern Virginia's climate cycles between humid summers, harsh winters, and significant freeze-thaw activity — conditions that cause traditional wood siding to rot and vinyl to warp and crack over time. James Hardie fiber cement doesn't expand, contract, or absorb moisture the way wood does, making it one of the best long-term investments for Arlington homes.

How quickly can you get to an Arlington project?

Arlington is well within our primary service area — our Virginia office is in McLean, which puts us about 10 minutes away. We can typically schedule a free inspection within a few days and have crews available on a timeline that works for your project. Emergency storm response is available when needed.
